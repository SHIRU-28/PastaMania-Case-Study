01The Setup

I flagged World Pasta Day while building our October content calendar and pitched a dedicated campaign. The client approved it to launch four exclusive, limited-time combo packs across all national branches:

  • 01 Treat For 1 Chicken Bolognese, Potato Wedges, Soft Drink
  • 02 Double Penne Creamy Chicken, Tuscan Pizza, Iced Tea Soda
  • 03 Fusilli Feast Carbonara, Chicken Bolognese, Margarita Pizza, Iced Tea
  • 04 Grande Platter Carbonara, Bolognese, Kocchii Cream, 2 Pizzas, Drinks

02The Reel Strategy

Instead of a standard, boring product promo, I engineered a high-retention guerrilla reel built on local hooks to maximize our organic reach:

1
The Hook

Began with a viral clip of a biker losing control, flying through the air, and smashing into a jeep's windshield to instantly break the user's scroll.

2
The Transition

Sourced our internal graphic designer—a university student with voiceover and discussion experience—to voice the biker character. He walked directly into the PastaMania branch while hilariously ranting about the crash in funny local slang, naturally showcasing the restaurant's premium interior layout.

3
The Payoff

He sat down at a table with the fresh combo dishes and added salt and pepper directly to the pasta, ending on a sensory close to drive cravings right before eating.

03The Community Piece

On October 24th at 9:00 AM, we partnered with the local Urban Council to host a private breakfast service at the restaurant for morning-shift street cleaners—the often-unappreciated local heroes.

Strategic Brand Positioning

We intentionally shot and edited this footage low-key with zero over-promotion to protect brand authenticity. If we over-promoted it, it would feel like the brand was exploiting a good deed for clout. Dropping this raw, cleanly graded content on the morning of October 25th set a genuine, community-first tone for the holiday right before we executed the evening retail push.

04My Role & Contributions

  • 1 Campaign Conceptualization: Flagged the retail opportunity in the October calendar and mapped out the initial framework during alignment meetings.
  • 2 Video Architecture & Direction: Structured timeline hooks, directed the on-site shoot, and handled post-production editing, lighting, and color grading.
  • 3 Talent & Script Management: Sourced internal voice talent and collaborated on comedic pacing and script delivery.
  • 4 Account Coordination: Managed fast-paced comms inside WhatsApp client groups and coordinated with operations and the Urban Council.
  • 5 Content Distribution: Scheduled emotional community video for peak morning traffic and lined up combo graphics for the evening push.
